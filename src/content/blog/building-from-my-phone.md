I'm writing this on my phone. My computer is running code in another room.

A year ago that sentence would have meant something different. A note, maybe. An email reply. Now it means I'm building something real.

Claude Code runs on my machine. I connect to it remotely, give it directions, review what it's doing, step in when it goes sideways. The computer does the building. I do the thinking. And I can do the thinking from anywhere: the metro, a waiting room, the ten minutes before a meeting starts.

I've been building this website in the gaps between other things. A layout fix over lunch. A project page while waiting in line. This post, started on a taxi ride. None of it would have happened if I needed a proper session at my desk. That session always ends up never coming.

What's changed isn't just where I can work. It's the threshold for starting. Before, anything worth building required a real block of time and a chair. Now the bar is: do I have five minutes and a thought? That's enough to move something forward. Over a week, things actually get built.

I work in AI consulting. I spend a lot of time thinking about how AI changes the way people and organisations work. But this particular shift, the ability to turn dead time into productive time without losing quality, feels like something people are still catching up to. It's not about squeezing more into every hour. It's that the gap between "I should build that" and "I actually built that" is smaller than it's ever been.

And the quality is real. That's the part that still surprises me. This isn't rough sketches that need redoing later. It's the actual thing, running, deployable, good. Claude Code doesn't just write code. It understands what I'm trying to build, handles the parts that don't need me, and lets me focus on the parts that do.

I don't think I'll go back to needing a dedicated block of time to build things. The constraint that used to gate most of my side projects, finding the time, has mostly dissolved. What's left is the harder part: having something worth building.
