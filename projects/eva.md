# EVA

## What it is

EVA is a voice-first AI companion designed specifically for elderly people.

It is not just a chatbot, but a system built around natural voice interaction, everyday support and trust.

The goal is simple: allow elderly users to speak with technology as if they were speaking to a person, without needing to learn how to use complex apps.

---

## Problem

Elderly people are often excluded from modern digital products.

- Apps are too complex  
- Interfaces rely on screens, menus and habits they don’t have  
- Technology creates anxiety and dependency instead of autonomy  

The problem is not their ability.

The problem is that technology is not designed for them.

The real challenge is not technical — it is usability, confidence and trust.

---

## Solution

EVA is built around a simple interaction model:

→ press one button and speak  

From there, EVA can:

- answer questions in natural language  
- create and manage reminders  
- help with daily context (e.g. “what do I have tomorrow?”)  
- initiate calls  
- explain information in a simple way  

The system combines voice interaction, AI decision-making and a backend that maintains context and actions.

It also considers a family layer to support setup and maintenance, without compromising user privacy.

---

## Why it matters

EVA is not trying to compete with general AI assistants.

It is focused on building a **high-trust, highly usable system for a specific group of users** that most products ignore.

This shifts the challenge from “what AI can do” to:

→ what people actually use and trust in real life

## What I’m learning

- Voice UX is extremely sensitive to latency and clarity  
- Simplicity matters more than functionality  
- Trust can be broken by small failures  
- Designing for elderly users requires a completely different approach  
- Validation in real environments matters more than technical progress
